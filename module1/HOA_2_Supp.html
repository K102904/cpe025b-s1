Halo-Halo: A vibrant and refreshing Filipino dessert

Halo-Halo is a classic Filipino dessert known for its vibrant colors and refreshing taste. This delightful treat combines various ingredients like beans, fruits, and ice cream in a sweet syrup.

"Halo-Halo is more than just a dessert; it's a celebration of Filipino flavors and traditions."

- Chef Maria, Filipino Cuisine Expert

Ingredients:

For the minatamis na saging (sweetened bananas) and tapioca:

  • Small tapioca pearls (optional): 1/4 Cup
  • Saba bananas: 3 pieces
  • Light brown sugar: 3/4 cup
  • Kosher salt: 1/4 teaspoon
  • Vanilla extract: 1/4 teaspoon

    • For Assembly:

  • Jackfruit in syrup: 1 can
  • Canned full-fat unsweetened coconut milk: 2/3 cup
  • Evaporated Milk: 3 tablespoons
  • Sweetened condensed milk: 3 tablespoons
  • Minatamis na saging and tapioca: (recipe above)
  • Coconut or ube ice cream: 4 scoops
  • Macapuno (coconut sport strings): 1/2 cup
  • Ube halaya: 1/2 cup
  • Nata de coco: 1 1/2 cups
  • Shaved Ice, plus more: 4 cups

Instructions:

  1. Cook the tapioca (optional).
  2. Slice the bananas.
  3. Make the syrup.
  4. Add the bananas
  5. Add the tapioca.
  6. Cut the jackfruit.
  7. Make the milk mixture.
  8. Assemble the halo-halo.
  9. Serve and enjoy.

Feel free to use whatever mix-ins you like best. In the Philippines, there are seemingly infinite halo-halo combos. Beans, such as garbanzo, adzuki, or mung beans, in syrup show up, as well as corn treated in the same way. Adding diced mango, diced sweet potato, large tapioca or sago pearls, and plain sliced bananas are also common. However you fill up your glass is up to you.
